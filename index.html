About Me

I work as an Architect Scrum Master within the Funding and Finance Technology division of Morgan Stanley. I joined the firm in 2016 as part of the 'Return to Work' programme, and have been leading agile teams across a range of projects, most recently in the re-architecture of our core finance systems.

I drive timely delivery and high client engagement in complex, interdependent systems and processes, bridging what can be a gap between end users and developers, especially within core infrastructure platforms.

I started my career with a PhD in Chemistry and spent two years in Japan researching liquid crystals at the Osaka National Research Institute. Over time, I found my niche in more client-focused and business development roles, working with clients and developers to deliver the business need — most recently at a boutique software house providing market data and analytics platforms to asset managers, hedge funds, and banks.

Advocacy & Mentoring

Within Morgan Stanley, I am a strong advocate of flexible part-time working based around school hours, and I act as a mentor to other returners, easing the transition back to the workplace.

Having taken four years out myself, I understand the challenges of returning to work and feel passionate about supporting others on the same journey. My experience with the Morgan Stanley Return To Work programme drives that commitment.
